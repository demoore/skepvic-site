Why Join

The purpose of this club is to promote scientific inquiry and critical thinking. As such, we are open to discussing and investigating any skeptically relevant topic which our members find interesting. Club members are encouraged to practice critical thinking skills and look for actual scientific evidence for and against any claims they come across.

The environment we aim to create is one in which people feel comfortable asking questions when they are unsure of whether or not they should accept a position, claim, or belief. Everyone is wrong sometimes and our goal is not to mock people for making mistakes or being fooled into believing silly things. That said, as skeptics, we also don't pretend to validate bogus claims in order to spare people's feelings. If a topic is being discussed by the group, it will be discussed openly and honestly. This will sometimes mean listening views that may oppose your own. Sometimes these discussions may even get pretty heated. We see this as a good thing. Skepticism is about changing your mind when good evidence is shown and a good skeptic knows when to admit they were wrong.

SkepVics Constitution

ARTICLE I: Name and Purpose

Section I: Name

This organization will be known as "SkepVics".

Section II: Purpose

The purpose of the organization shall be:

  1. To promote scientific inquiry and critical thinking
  2. To provide a safe and welcoming community for skeptics and those interested in learning more about skepticism as a movement.
  3. To organize events and activities that are of interest to skeptics.

ARTICLE II: Membership

Section I: Membership Requirements

Membership will be open to all University of Victoria students, faculty, staff and community members with no less than two thirds of the members of a club shall be UVic students.

Section II: Expulsion

Members of SkepVics may be expelled if they (1) violate any of the terms of the UVSS Harassment policy or (2) have engages or are engaging in activities that are or have the potential to be detrimental to the interests of SkepVics. The decision to expel a member in the latter case (2) will be decided via an anonymous majority vote open to all active club members. Members who are expelled may not apply to join SkepVics again for a period of twelve months. Members who are expelled lose any position they have held within SkepVics

Section III: Membership Fees

There shall be no fee associated with membership with SkepVics.


ARTICLE III: Governance

Section I: Election of Executives

SkepVics will no less than three executives; at least two of whom will be UVic students. The executive will consist of two Co-presidents and a Treasurer. The executive will be elected by the membership of the club once per year. Election shall occur by secret ballot at an Annual General Meeting, or at a meeting scheduled for the express purpose of election. The candidates with the most votes win; uncontested candidates win by default. No members of the club shall be denied the right to vote in the election of the executive of the club.The term of office for each elected executive shall be one year with the exception of members who either resign or are expelled before the end of their term.

Section II: Executive Duties

The two Co-presidents of SkepVics are expected to fulfill the following duties:

  1. Run weekly meetings
  2. Manage the group email account and online presence
  3. Maintain the integrity and identity of the group
  4. Be the primary representative of SkepVics
  5. Ensure the future of the group
  6. Allocates duties as necessary

The treasurer of SkepVics is expected to fulfill the following duties:

  1. Be responsible for all financial matters
  2. Be responsible for keeping records and possession (on behalf of the group) of all merchandise
  3. Keep the organization current with all paperwork that needs to be filed with the University of Victory

ARTICLE IV: Membership Meetings

Section I: Regular Meetings

Regular meetings of SkepVics shall be held on a biweekly basis. Additional meetings may be scheduled as required by any elected executive.

Section II: Business Meetings

Business meetings shall occur at the discretion of the elected officers. Advance notice of at least 48 hours should be given to all members.

Section III: Annual Meeting

An Annual General Meeting will be held once each September. The purposes of this meeting will be to hold an election for the executive which will consist of two Co-presidents and a Treasurer, to discuss and review any changes to the SkepVics constitution that have been proposed/made over the preceding year, and to generally review the events of the preceding year.


ARTICLE V: Amendment

Any member may propose a constitutional amendment. Members shall be notified at least 48 hours in advance of a pending vote on an amendment and will be made aware of the location of such a meeting. Any member present at such a meeting may vote on an amendment. The amendment shall be read aloud and discussed on the day of the vote before ballots are collected. The constitution may be amended by a majority vote of no less than two-thirds from a voting pool of no less than ten members
